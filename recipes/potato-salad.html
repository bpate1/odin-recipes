Potato Salad

Description

Southern potato salad is a beloved classic in Southern American cuisine, featuring tender, boiled potatoes mixed with a creamy dressing of mayo, mustard, and tangy apple cider vinegar or pickle juice. Additional ingredients like hard-boiled eggs, pickles, onions, and sometimes bacon add texture and flavor. Seasoned with salt, pepper, and a hint of paprika or cayenne, this comforting side dish embodies Southern hospitality and is perfect for gatherings and barbecues, delivering a creamy and satisfying experience that complements a variety of main dishes.

Ingredients

  • Potatoes
  • Mayonnaise
  • Mustard
  • Apple Cider Vinegar or Pickle Juice
  • Hard-boiled Eggs
  • Pickles
  • Onions
  • Celery
  • Green Onions
  • Bacon
  • Salt
  • Black Pepper
  • Paprika or Cayenne Pepper

Steps

  1. Boil the potatoes: Place the potatoes in a large pot, cover them with water, and bring to a boil. Cook the potatoes until they are fork-tender, about 15-20 minutes. Drain and let them cool completely.
  2. Prepare the dressing: In a separate bowl, mix together the mayonnaise, mustard, and apple cider vinegar or pickle juice. Stir until well combined, creating a creamy dressing.
  3. Dice the potatoes: Once the potatoes are cooled, peel them (optional) and dice them into bite-sized pieces. Place the diced potatoes in a large mixing bowl.
  4. Add the ingredients: To the diced potatoes, add the chopped hard-boiled eggs, pickles or pickle relish, onions, celery (if using), green onions (if using), and crumbled bacon (if using).
  5. Season the salad: Pour the dressing over the potato mixture, gently tossing everything together to coat the potatoes and ingredients evenly. Season with salt and black pepper to taste.
  6. Chill and serve: Cover the bowl with plastic wrap and refrigerate the potato salad for at least an hour to allow the flavors to meld. Before serving, sprinkle paprika or cayenne pepper (if using) on top for a touch of spice.
  7. Enjoy: Serve the chilled Southern potato salad as a delicious side dish alongside your favorite Southern meals, grilled meats, or at picnics and barbecues.
