Collard Greens

Description

Southern collard greens are a beloved and hearty dish in Southern cuisine. Made from the sturdy, dark green leaves of the collard plant, they are slow-cooked with smoky ingredients like bacon, along with onions, garlic, and chili peppers for added flavor. The result is a rich and comforting taste. Often served as a side dish, collard greens hold cultural significance, reflecting a connection to African American heritage and the resourcefulness of the past. Whether enjoyed at family gatherings or festive occasions, they bring warmth and a sense of history to Southern tables.

Ingredients

  • Collard Greens
  • Bacon
  • Onion
  • Garlic
  • Chicken Broth
  • Salt
  • Pepper

Steps

  1. Prepare the collard greens: Thoroughly wash the collard greens under cold running water. Remove any tough stems and ribs from the leaves, then stack the leaves on top of each other and slice them into thick strips.
  2. Cook the meat: In a large pot or Dutch oven over medium heat, cook the bacon until it releases its fat and starts to brown. This step adds the smoky and savory flavors to the dish.
  3. Add the onions and garlic: Add the chopped onions to the pot with the meat and cook until they become translucent. Stir in the minced garlic and chopped chili peppers (if using) and cook for another minute or so until fragrant.
  4. Add the collard greens: Place the sliced collard greens into the pot, stirring to coat them with the meat and onion mixture. The greens may seem voluminous at first, but they will wilt down as they cook.
  5. Pour in the liquid: Pour in the chicken broth or water, covering the collard greens. The liquid will help to simmer and cook the greens.
  6. Season the collard greens: Season the mixture with salt and pepper to taste. You can also add any additional spices or seasonings you prefer at this stage.
  7. Simmer the collard greens: Bring the pot to a boil, then reduce the heat to low. Cover the pot and let the collard greens simmer for about 1 to 1.5 hours or until they are tender and fully cooked. The cooking time may vary based on the toughness of the greens and the size of the leaves.
  8. Check for doneness: Test the collard greens for doneness by tasting a piece. They should be tender but still have a slight bite.
  9. Serve: Once the collard greens are fully cooked and tender, they are ready to be served. You can enjoy them as a side dish alongside your favorite Southern meals or as a main course with some cornbread on the side.
